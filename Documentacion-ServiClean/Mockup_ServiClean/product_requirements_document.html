Executive Summary** This document outlines the requirements for the "Personalized Workout & Habit Coach" feature within the FitFlow mobile application. The primary goal of this feature is to significantly improve user engagement, retention, and workout consistency by offering dynamic, AI-driven workout recommendations and integrating habit-forming nudges. This addresses a key user pain point around workout discovery fatigue and lack of consistent motivation, ultimately driving higher daily/weekly active users and increasing the lifetime value of our subscribers. --- **2. Problem Statement** Our existing FitFlow users, particularly those beyond their first month, exhibit a significant drop-off in engagement and workout completion. Data shows: * **Discovery Fatigue:** 40% of users report difficulty finding suitable workouts after completing introductory plans. * **Inconsistent Usage:** Average weekly workout completion drops by 25% after week 4 for active users. * **Lack of Motivation/Guidance:** Survey data indicates users struggle with motivation and often feel unsure of "what to do next" to progress. * **High Churn:** Our 6-week churn rate stands at 30%, with qualitative feedback suggesting a lack of personalized pathways contributes significantly. This results in a suboptimal user experience, reduced subscription renewals, and lower overall LTV. --- **3. Goals & KPIs** **Primary Goals:** 1. Increase user engagement and retention on the FitFlow platform. 2. Empower users to build sustainable fitness habits. **Key Performance Indicators (KPIs):** * **Increase Weekly Active Users (WAU):** +15% within 3 months of launch. * **Increase Weekly Workout Completion Rate:** +20% for users engaging with the feature. * **Decrease 6-Week Churn Rate:** -10% for users actively using the Personalized Coach. * **Improve In-App NPS/CSAT:** +5 points for users interacting with the feature. * **Increase Feature Adoption Rate:** 50% of active users engage with the coach within 4 weeks of launch. --- **4. Target Audience** This feature is primarily designed for: * **New Users (0-4 weeks):** To provide immediate, structured guidance and prevent early churn. * **Intermediate Users (4+ weeks):** To offer progression, variety, and sustained motivation, addressing discovery fatigue. * **Users seeking consistency:** Individuals who struggle with habit formation and need external nudges and personalized planning. * **Fitness Enthusiasts:** Who desire advanced, adaptive programming tailored to their evolving goals. --- **5. Solution Overview: Personalized Workout & Habit Coach** The "Personalized Workout & Habit Coach" will be an integrated module within the FitFlow app that: 1. **Assesses User Profile:** Gathers initial fitness level, goals, available equipment, and time constraints. 2. **Generates Adaptive Workout Plans:** Creates dynamic, multi-week workout plans tailored to the user's profile, adapting based on their progress and feedback. 3. **Provides Daily Recommendations:** Suggests workouts or activities based on recent activity, planned schedule, and AI-driven insights. 4. **Integrates Habit-Forming Nudges:** Utilizes push notifications, in-app reminders, and streak tracking to encourage consistency. 5. **Offers Progress Tracking & Visualization:** Allows users to track their journey, celebrate milestones, and visualize their improvements. 6. **Gathers Continuous Feedback:** Allows users to rate workouts and provide input to refine future recommendations. --- **6. Key Features (Detailed Requirements)** **6.1. Onboarding & Profile Assessment** * **Requirement:** Users complete an initial survey (fitness level, goals, preferred workout types, available equipment, time availability, injury history). * **Acceptance Criteria:** * All mandatory fields are clearly indicated. * User can skip/complete later (with default recommendations). * Data is stored and accessible by the recommendation engine. **6.2. Dynamic Workout Recommendation Engine (AI/ML Driven)** * **Requirement:** The system recommends full workout plans (e.g., 4-week program) or daily individual workouts. * **Acceptance Criteria:** * Recommendations are based on user profile, past workout performance, and explicit feedback. * Recommendations consider workout variations, progressive overload principles, and recovery days. * Engine can recommend workouts from the existing FitFlow library and potentially external sources (e.g., bodyweight, yoga). * Users can "refresh" recommendations if they don't like the current suggestion. **6.3. Adaptive Workout Plan Generation & Management** * **Requirement:** Users can commit to a multi-week personalized plan. * **Acceptance Criteria:** * Plan dynamically adjusts based on completed workouts, skipped workouts, and user-reported feedback (e.g., "too easy," "too hard"). * Users can view their weekly plan overview. * Users can swap individual workouts within their plan for suitable alternatives. * Users can pause or reschedule their plan. **6.4. Habit-Forming Nudges & Reminders** * **Requirement:** System proactively reminds and motivates users. * **Acceptance Criteria:** * **Push Notifications:** Customizable workout reminders (e.g., "Time for your workout!"), streak milestones (e.g., "3-day streak!"), encouraging messages (e.g., "Keep up the great work!"). * **In-App Badges/Streaks:** Visual indicators of consistency (e.g., "7-day workout streak," "Completed 10 workouts"). * **Weekly Progress Reports:** Summaries of completed workouts, time spent, calories burned (if available). **6.5. In-App Progress Tracking & Visualization** * **Requirement:** Users can easily see their fitness journey and achievements. * **Acceptance Criteria:** * Dashboard view displaying upcoming workout, current streak, and overall plan progress. * Visual charts for workout frequency, duration, and personal bests over time. * Achievement badges for completing milestones (e.g., "First Plan Completed," "50 Workouts"). **6.6. User Feedback Loop** * **Requirement:** Users can provide feedback on individual workouts and recommendations. * **Acceptance Criteria:** * Post-workout rating (e.g., 1-5 stars) and optional free-text comments. * "Too easy/too hard" feedback option during a workout or immediately after. * This feedback is fed back into the recommendation engine. --- **7. User Stories (Examples)** * **As a new FitFlow user**, I want to easily set up my fitness profile, so the app can suggest workouts tailored to my current level and goals. * **As a busy professional**, I want to receive a push notification reminding me to do my planned workout, so I stay consistent and don't forget. * **As someone looking to get stronger**, I want the app to suggest progressively challenging workouts, so I can see continuous improvement. * **As an active FitFlow user**, I want to see a visual representation of my workout streak and progress, so I feel motivated to continue. * **As a user who prefers specific types of workouts**, I want to be able to tell the app my preferences, so the recommendations are always relevant. --- **8. Technical Considerations & Assumptions** * **ML Integration:** Requires significant backend development for the recommendation engine (data pipelines, model training, serving). * **API Development:** New APIs required for profile assessment, plan generation, feedback submission, and data retrieval for UI. * **Data Storage:** Expansion of user data models to store preferences, progress, and feedback. * **Push Notification Service:** Leverage existing or integrate new push notification provider. * **Mobile Platform Support:** Feature must be fully functional on both iOS and Android. * **Backend Scalability:** The recommendation engine must be scalable to handle a growing user base. * **Security & Privacy:** All user data handled in compliance with GDPR, CCPA, etc. --- **9. Dependencies & Risks** **Dependencies:** * **Data Science Team:** For building and tuning the ML recommendation engine. * **Design Team:** For UI/UX flows, wireframes, and high-fidelity mockups. * **Existing Workout Library:** Relies on the quality and breadth of our current workout content. * **Backend Infrastructure:** Requires sufficient capacity and performance. * **Analytics Tracking:** Need robust event tracking setup for KPIs. **Risks:** * **Recommendation Engine Accuracy:** If recommendations aren't truly personalized or effective, user trust will be lost. (Mitigation: Extensive A/B testing, user feedback loops, iterative model improvements). * **Data Availability:** Lack of sufficient quality user data for ML model training. (Mitigation: Phased rollout, initial rule-based recommendations, focus on data collection). * **User Adoption:** Users may not engage with a new feature. (Mitigation: Strong in-app onboarding, clear value proposition, marketing push). * **Scope Creep:** Feature requests extending beyond core functionality. (Mitigation: Strict adherence to PRD, clear "Out of Scope" section). * **Performance Issues:** Slow load times or buggy interactions due to complex logic. (Mitigation: Thorough testing, performance monitoring, phased rollout). --- **10. Success Metrics & Measurement Plan** * **Dashboard:** Create a dedicated product dashboard in Mixpanel/Amplitude/Google Analytics to track all relevant KPIs. * **Event Tracking:** * `coach_onboarding_completed` * `plan_generated` * `workout_recommended` * `workout_accepted` * `workout_skipped` * `workout_completed` * `workout_rated` * `notification_received` * `notification_opened` * `streak_achieved` * **Surveys:** Regular in-app surveys to gather qualitative feedback on the coach's effectiveness and user satisfaction. * **A/B Testing:** Key UI/UX elements and recommendation algorithms will be A/B tested to optimize for engagement and retention. --- **11. Out of Scope** * Integration with wearable devices (e.g., Apple Watch, Fitbit) for real-time heart rate or activity tracking (Phase 2). * Live personal coaching or direct chat with human trainers. * Community-based workout challenges tied directly to the coach. * Advanced nutrition tracking or meal planning integration. * Gamified leaderboards among friends. --- **12. Open Questions & Future Considerations** * **ML Model Sourcing:** Will we build this entirely in-house, or explore third-party ML services? * **UI/UX Specifics:** Exact placement of the "Coach" entry point in the app navigation. * **Data Privacy Consent:** How will we explicitly gain user consent for using their data for personalized recommendations? * **Localization:** What languages and cultural nuances need to be considered for prompts and recommendations? * **Phase 2 Ideas:** Smart recovery days based on external data, integration with wearable health data, AI-powered form feedback. --- **13.
